About Alex Jassen

Alex is an associate professor of Hebrew and Judaic Studies at New York University. He has a Ph.D. in Hebrew and Judaic Studies and has published extensively on the Dead Sea Scrolls and ancient Judaism. He is a member of the international editorial team responsible for the publication of the Dead Sea Scrolls and has received numerous awards for his work, including the John Templeton Award for Theological Promise. He is also a co-editor-in-chief of the Journal of Ancient Judaism and has served as an academic advisor for The Dead Sea Scrolls exhibit at the Science Museum of Minnesota. Currently, he is working on a book on religious violence in the Dead Sea Scrolls and ancient Judaism and has received a fellowship from the National Endowment for the Humanities for his work on this topic.

What is an FFIR

The Faculty Fellow-In-Residence (FFIR) program creates vibrant and engaging learning communities for students within the residence halls as a way of integrating students’ academic experiences with their residential lives. The program offers students the benefits of “small college” life and is committed to advancing this goal through the FFIR program. The FFIR(s) in your building will be in charge of things like hosting fun events, coffee hours, study breaks, co-curricular activities, tours and excursions, community service projects, competitions, and guest speaker programs to help foster these crucial relationships in a critical moment in a student’s development.
